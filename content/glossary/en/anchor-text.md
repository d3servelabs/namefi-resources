**Anchor text** is the human-readable, clickable portion of a hyperlink — the underlined words a visitor sees rather than the raw URL. Search engines treat anchor text as a strong relevance signal: when hundreds of sites link to a domain using the phrase "cheap flight insurance," that pattern tells Google the destination page is authoritative on that topic, directly influencing its [domain authority](/en/glossary/domain-authority/) for related queries. The anchor text profile of a domain therefore shapes its footprint across every [SERP](/en/glossary/serp/) where it competes. Over-optimized anchor text — for example, an unnatural concentration of exact-match commercial phrases from low-quality sources — can trigger algorithmic penalties, which is why link-building strategy favors diverse, editorial anchors. When a [301 redirect](/en/glossary/301-redirect/) is in place, most link equity, including the anchor signal from those inbound links, passes through to the destination URL. For domain investors evaluating an aged name, auditing its anchor text distribution is a standard due-diligence step: a clean, topically coherent profile is a genuine SEO asset; a spammy one may carry hidden ranking risk. *Source: Google Search Central — Crawlable Links.*
