**AML** (*Anti-Money-Laundering*) is the ongoing transaction screening and reporting that regulated financial and crypto services use to detect and deter illicit funds — monitoring activity against sanctions lists and reporting unusual patterns to authorities such as FinCEN in the US. Where [KYC](/en/glossary/kyc/) verifies who a user is at onboarding, AML watches what they do afterward. In crypto, regulated [stablecoin](/en/glossary/stablecoin/) issuers and custodial services can freeze or blacklist [custodial-ownership](/en/glossary/custodial-ownership/) accounts that fail these compliance checks, while non-custodial protocols generally have no party able to enforce them.
