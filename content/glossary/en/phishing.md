**Phishing** is a social-engineering attack in which criminals impersonate a trusted entity — a bank, [registrar](/en/glossary/registrar/), or [marketplace](/en/glossary/marketplace/) — through deceptive emails, messages, or lookalike websites to steal credentials, seed phrases, or funds. Domain abuse is central to most phishing campaigns: attackers register convincing lookalike names via [typosquatting](/en/glossary/typosquatting/) or homograph tricks, then point them at fraudulent pages mimicking the real site. A successful phish against a registrar account is a common precursor to [domain hijacking](/en/glossary/domain-hijacking/), and [DNS hijacking](/en/glossary/dns-hijacking/) can weaponize a legitimate domain for phishing without the owner noticing.
