A **premium domain** is a name considered especially valuable — short, memorable, brandable, or built on a strong keyword — and therefore priced well above standard registration rates. "Premium" comes in two flavors: **registry premiums**, where the [registry](/en/glossary/registry/) itself sets an elevated price (and often an elevated [renewal](/en/glossary/domain-renewal/)) on a name within its [TLD](/en/glossary/tld/), and **[aftermarket](/en/glossary/aftermarket/) premiums**, where an owner resells a name through [domain trading](/en/glossary/domain-trading/) for far more than they paid. Premium status is about market demand, not technical difference — the name resolves like any other.
