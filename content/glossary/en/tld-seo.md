**TLD SEO impact** refers to the degree to which a domain's extension — .com, .io, .ai, a country-code [ccTLD](/en/glossary/cctld/), or a [new gTLD](/en/glossary/new-gtld/) like .shop — influences its organic search rankings. Google has consistently stated that all [gTLDs](/en/glossary/gtld/) are treated equally for ranking purposes, and that content quality, site authority, and relevant backlinks dwarf the extension as a ranking factor. That said, there are two real nuances: country-code TLDs (e.g., .de, .fr) can receive a geographic boost in local [SERP](/en/glossary/serp/) results because Google uses them as a geotargeting signal, and user trust — which affects click-through rate — still skews toward familiar extensions. A .com carrying strong [domain authority](/en/glossary/domain-authority/) will almost always outrank an identical site on an unfamiliar extension, not because Google scores the TLD higher, but because .com has decades of earned brand trust that feeds indirectly into engagement metrics. The practical takeaway is clear: a premium .com or established [ccTLD](/en/glossary/cctld/) commands a valuation premium partly because of these trust effects, even if TLD alone is not a direct ranking lever.
