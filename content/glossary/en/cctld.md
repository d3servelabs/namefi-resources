A **ccTLD (country-code top-level domain)** is a two-letter [TLD](/en/glossary/tld/) assigned to a country or territory based on the ISO 3166 list — `.uk`, `.de`, `.jp`, `.io` (British Indian Ocean Territory), and so on. Unlike a [gTLD](/en/glossary/gtld/), a ccTLD is administered under the authority of its country rather than directly by [ICANN](/en/glossary/icann/) contract, so each one sets its own eligibility, pricing, and policy rules — some require local presence, others (like `.io` or `.co`) sell globally.
