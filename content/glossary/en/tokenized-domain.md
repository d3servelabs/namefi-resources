A **tokenized domain** is a conventional DNS domain — like `example.com` — whose ownership has been bridged onto a blockchain by [minting](/en/glossary/minting/) an [NFT](/en/glossary/nft/) that serves as the cryptographic deed. The domain remains fully functional in the global DNS; the token simply adds a parallel, blockchain-enforced ownership layer. Whoever holds the NFT in their [wallet](/en/glossary/wallet/) controls the domain: they can sell it peer-to-peer on any NFT marketplace, collateralize it in a DeFi protocol, or transfer it in seconds without filing a registrar transfer request. The binding between the DNS record and the on-chain token is maintained by a smart contract that syncs [tokenize](/en/glossary/tokenize/) events with a compliant registrar. [Domain ownership](/en/glossary/domain-ownership/) disputes that once required UDRP proceedings become more transparent because the chain of custody is permanently auditable on-chain. Tokenized domains give domain investors, brands, and developers the composability of crypto assets without sacrificing the utility of the traditional web.
