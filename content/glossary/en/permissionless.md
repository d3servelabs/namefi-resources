**Permissionless** refers to systems that allow anyone to participate without requiring approval from a central authority. In [blockchain](/en/glossary/blockchain/) contexts, permissionless networks let users transact, build applications, or interact with protocols without gatekeepers. Traditional domain management requires permission from [registrars](/en/glossary/registrar/) and follows their policies, but [tokenized domains](/en/glossary/tokenized-domain/) enable permissionless transfers and integrations. Once you own a tokenized domain, you can trade it, use it in [DeFi](/en/glossary/defi/) protocols, or build applications around it without seeking approval from intermediaries—as long as the underlying blockchain network is accessible to you.
