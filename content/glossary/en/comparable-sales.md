**Comparable sales** — or *comps* — are the historical sale prices of domains that closely resemble the name being valued, and they are the single most reliable input in any [domain appraisal](/en/glossary/domain-appraisal/). A comp is most useful when it matches on TLD, length, word count, keyword category, and approximate age of the sale; a three-letter .com that sold for $80,000 last year is a far stronger signal for a similar three-letter .com than a five-year-old sale of a four-word .net. Databases like NameBio aggregate hundreds of thousands of disclosed aftermarket transactions, and platforms such as DNJournal publish weekly sale reports, making the [aftermarket](/en/glossary/aftermarket/) increasingly transparent. Because not all domain sales are disclosed, comps always represent a floor sample rather than a complete market picture.
