The **URS** (Uniform Rapid Suspension) is an [ICANN](/en/glossary/icann/)-mandated dispute mechanism designed to handle the most clear-cut cases of [trademark](/en/glossary/trademark/) infringement faster and more cheaply than the full [UDRP](/en/glossary/udrp/) process. Rather than transferring a domain to the complainant, a successful URS action suspends the domain for the remainder of its registration term — nameservers are redirected to an ICANN-controlled landing page, effectively neutralizing the harm. To prevail, the trademark owner must show that the domain is identical or confusingly similar to their mark, that the registrant has no legitimate rights, and that the domain was registered and is being used in bad faith — the same three elements as the UDRP, but evaluated at a summary level. URS was introduced alongside [new gTLD](/en/glossary/new-gtld/) launches to help brand owners manage potential [cybersquatting](/en/glossary/cybersquatting/) at scale across hundreds of new extensions.
