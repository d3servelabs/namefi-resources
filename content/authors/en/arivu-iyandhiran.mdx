Arivu Iyandhiran (அறிவு இயந்திரன்) is a translator in his late twenties, based
in Coimbatore. He started out as an automation technician on a textile-mill
floor, began blogging about technology in Tamil and English, and turned that
into full-time localization work.

He cares about Tamil that reads as Tamil rather than English wearing a Tamil
script, and about the choices — transliteration, script, register — that decide
whether a technical article feels native or imported. Filter coffee, Carnatic
playlists, and weekend kabaddi round out the week.

For Namefi he localizes articles on domains and naming into Tamil — handling
Tamil-script IDNs, transliteration, and regional namespaces, so a name reads
well in தமிழ் and in English alike.
