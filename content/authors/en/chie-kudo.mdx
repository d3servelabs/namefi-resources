Chie Kudō (工藤 知恵) is a translator in her thirties, based in Fukuoka. She
worked as a hardware QA engineer at an electronics maker, writing and checking
product documentation, before moving into localizing technical and editorial
writing between English and Japanese.

QA habits carry over: she is exacting about consistent terminology and about the
details unique to Japanese typesetting — kanji versus kana versus romaji for a
brand, half-width and full-width characters, and wasei-eigo that means something
different from the English it borrows. She keeps a small balcony vegetable
garden and boulders on weekends.

For Namefi she localizes articles on domains and naming into Japanese, with an
eye for how a name reads on the page and whether it will be typed correctly the
first time.
