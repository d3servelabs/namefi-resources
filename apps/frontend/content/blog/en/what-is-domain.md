# What is a Domain Name?

A domain name is the address of your website on the Internet. It is what users type into their browser to visit your site. Think of it as your digital property—a unique identifier that helps people find your online presence.

## Why are domain names important?
- Build your online brand
- Make your website easy to find
- Increase credibility and trust
- Portable and flexible
- Universal naming method
- Works across all devices and platforms
- Business value
- Can be a valuable business asset
- Helps with SEO
- Protects your brand

## How do domains work?
Domains work through the DNS (Domain Name System). Here's a simple explanation:
- Users enter a domain (e.g., example.com)
- The browser queries DNS servers for the address
- DNS servers resolve the domain to an IP address
- The browser connects to the web server
- The website is displayed

## Common TLDs
- .com (commercial)
- .org (organization)
- .net (network)
- .io (technology)
- .xyz (generic)

## Country Code TLDs
- .cn (China)
- .us (United States)
- .uk (United Kingdom)
- .jp (Japan)

## Registration and Management
- Domains are registered with registrars
- Must be unique
- Can contain letters, numbers, and hyphens
- Managed through control panels

## Summary
A domain name is your address on the Internet. It's essential for branding, credibility, and making your website accessible to everyone.
